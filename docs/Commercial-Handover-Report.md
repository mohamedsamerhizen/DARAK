# DARAK Commercial Handover Report

## Purpose

DARAK is a commercial-grade residential compound management backend. This handover document summarizes the product scope, buyer-facing value, operational responsibilities, and remaining production decisions before a paid deployment.

## Product Scope

DARAK currently covers:

- Identity, JWT authentication, refresh-token hardening and role-based access.
- Compound, building, floor, unit and parking structure.
- Resident profiles, occupancy, ownership and emergency/family information.
- Utility billing, payments, rent and installment workflows.
- Meter readings and commercial billing-rule extensions.
- Visitors, maintenance, complaints, violations, fines and work orders.
- Admin/resident communication, notification outbox and commercial campaigns.
- Documents, document requirements, approval/rejection and expiry tracking.
- Approval workflow for sensitive admin operations.
- Resident risk flags and operational command center.
- Financial control center, ledger, adjustments, aging and revenue reports.
- Audit logging and legal traceability.
- Support cases, SLA foundation and management reporting.
- System settings, license profile, maintenance mode, version, diagnostics, background job and integration-failure tracking.

## Buyer Value

DARAK is positioned as a backend platform for large residential compounds that need a single operational system for residents, finance, utilities, documents, communications and management reporting.

The strongest commercial value comes from:

1. Financial accountability: ledger, statements, adjustments, aging and revenue reporting.
2. Security and scope isolation: compound-scoped authorization and admin/resident separation.
3. Traceability: audit logs for sensitive operations and management events.
4. Operational control: command center, support cases, risk flags and SLA-oriented views.
5. Deployment readiness: environment-driven configuration, startup validation, system health and release scripts.

## Deployment Ownership

Before a paid deployment, the buyer or operator must decide:

- Hosting target: VPS, cloud VM, container platform or private server.
- SQL Server hosting and backup schedule.
- Email/SMS provider credentials and sending limits.
- Document storage location and retention policy.
- SuperAdmin account creation policy.
- Operational roles and compound assignment workflow.
- Commercial license terms and support SLA.

## Delivery Contents

A commercial delivery package should contain:

- Source code without `bin`, `obj`, `.env`, logs, uploads or ZIP artifacts.
- `.env.example` only, never a real `.env`.
- Database migration instructions.
- Deployment runbook.
- Buyer operations runbook.
- Production readiness checklist.
- Final release notes.
- Release governance and migration governance documents.
- Environment-variable reference.
- Build/test/EF verification evidence generated by the seller.

## Non-Negotiable Safety Rules

- Do not deliver real secrets inside source code.
- Do not deliver local upload files or logs.
- Do not disable startup security validation.
- Do not run production with placeholder JWT, SQL, SMS or Email credentials.
- Do not bypass compound-scoped authorization.
- Do not alter old routes during final packaging.

## Commercial Status

This version is suitable for buyer demonstration and controlled pilot deployment after the buyer supplies real infrastructure credentials and agrees on operational policies.
